State Route 372 (SR 372) is a very short, two-lane east-west state highway in the southern part of the U.S. state of Ohio.
The western terminus of State Route 372 is at a T-intersection with the concurrency of U.S. Route 23 (US 23) and State Route 104 (SR 104).
The western terminus of State Route 372 is about six and a half miles (10.5 km) north of Waverly.
The eastern terminus of State Route 372 is just 0.68 miles (1.09 km) to the east of the western terminus.
The eastern terminus of State Route 372 is at the boundary of Scioto Trail State Forest.
Stoney Creek Road continues east of the eastern terminus of State Route 372.
Stoney Creek Road traverses through Scioto Trail State Forest in the direction of the Scioto River.
The primary function of State Route 372 has been to provide access to Scioto Trail State Forest from the state highway system since its inception in the middle of the 1930s.
The entirety of State Route 372 is located within Franklin Township in rural southern Ross County.
State Route 372 begins at a T-intersection with the US 23/SR 104 concurrency.
State Route 372 travels easterly into a series of curves in the shape of an "S".
State Route 372 takes the route to the north and then bends to the southeast.
State Route 372 travels past an intersection with North Ridge Road.
State Route 372 abruptly comes to an end as it crosses the western boundary of Scioto Trail State Forest.
The roadway continues eastward from the end of State Route 372 as Stoney Creek Road.
The Ohio Department of Transportation (ODOT) regularly conducts surveys to determine the amount of traffic that utilizes its highway system.
The volumes measured in ODOT surveys are expressed in terms of average annual daily traffic (AADT).
Average annual daily traffic (AADT) represents the amount of traffic volume on a given segment of a highway on any average day of the year.
In 2010, ODOT determined that an average of 340 passenger vehicles per day used State Route 372.
In 2010, ODOT determined that an average of 20 commercial vehicles per day used State Route 372.
State Route 372 is not a part of the National Highway System.
The National Highway System is a network of roadways identified as being most important for the economy, mobility, and defense of the nation.
The SR 372 designation was established in 1934.
Throughout its entire history, State Route 372 has served to provide a connection to Scioto Trail State Forest from US 23.
When State Route 372 was first designated, all of State Route 372 was gravel-surfaced.
State Route 372 was paved by 1958.
The entire route of State Route 372 is in Franklin Township, Ross County.
