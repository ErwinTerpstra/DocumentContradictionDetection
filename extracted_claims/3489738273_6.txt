Blue Bell ice cream has temporarily shut down one of its manufacturing plants.
The shutdown is due to the discovery of listeria contamination in a serving of ice cream.
The contaminated ice cream originated from the Broken Arrow, Oklahoma, plant.
Public health officials warned consumers not to eat any Blue Bell-branded products made at the Broken Arrow plant.
The warning includes 3-ounce servings of Blue Bell ice cream from the Broken Arrow plant.
The 3-ounce servings went to institutions in containers marked with the letters O, P, Q, R, S, or T behind the coding date.
The warning by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention does not affect other Blue Bell ice cream.
The warning does not affect other 3-ounce servings not made at the Broken Arrow plant.
Blue Bell has recalled other products.
Blue Bell is shutting down the Broken Arrow facility out of an abundance of caution.
Blue Bell is searching for a possible cause of contamination.
This is the third time Blue Bell has taken action due to a listeria outbreak at a Kansas hospital.
Listeria monocytogenes was found in a cup of ice cream recovered from the Kansas hospital.
The contaminated cup was produced at the Broken Arrow plant in April 2014.
The CDC found listeria bacteria in additional samples of the same product recovered from the Broken Arrow plant.
The bacteria in the hospital sample and the factory sample appeared to match each other genetically.
The bacteria in the hospital sample did not appear identical to listeria samples taken from patients infected in the Kansas outbreak.
The CDC found listeria samples taken from patients in Texas matched the listeria in recovered samples.
Patients in Texas came down with listeriosis between 2010 and 2014.
The patients in Texas were in a hospital that served 3-ounce Blue Bell cups.
None of this means the ice cream is the source of either outbreak of infections.
The investigation to determine whether these illnesses are related to exposure to Blue Bell products is ongoing.
In early March, Blue Bell recalled a group of products made at a plant in Texas.
Blue Bell later added 3-ounce cup servings to the recall.
Five people were infected with listeria in Kansas in the past year.
Three people died in Kansas from listeria that might be linked to Blue Bell Creameries products.
All five infected individuals were hospitalized at the same hospital before developing listeriosis.
At least four of the infected individuals had consumed milkshakes made with Blue Bell ice cream before developing the infection.
Paul Kruse is the CEO and president of Blue Bell.
Paul Kruse said in a statement that Blue Bell has to be and can be better than this.
Quality and safety have always been top priorities for Blue Bell.
Blue Bell is deeply saddened and concerned for all those who have been affected.
The CDC advises individuals and institutions to check their freezers for the recalled products.
The CDC advises individuals and institutions to throw away the recalled products.
Blue Bell stated that the recall does not include Blue Bell ice cream half gallons, pints, quarts, 3 gallons, or other 3-ounce cups.
This is the first product recall in the 108-year history of Blue Bell Creameries.
Listeriosis is a serious infection caused by eating food contaminated with listeria.
Listeriosis primarily affects the elderly, pregnant women, newborns, and people with weakened immune systems.
Symptoms of a listeria infection include fever and muscle aches.
Symptoms of a listeria infection can be associated with diarrhea or other gastrointestinal symptoms.
In the United States, an estimated 1,600 people become seriously ill each year due to listeria.
Approximately 16% of listeria illnesses result in death.
Cervical infections caused by listeriosis in pregnant women may result in stillbirth or spontaneous abortion during the second or third trimesters.
