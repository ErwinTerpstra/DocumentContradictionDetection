King's Highway 89 is an east-west provincially maintained highway in the southwestern portion of the Canadian province of Ontario.
King's Highway 89 stretches 107 kilometres from the junction of Highway 9 and Highway 23 in Harriston in the west to Highway 400 just east of Cookstown in the east.
The principal urban centres along Highway 89 include Alliston, Shelburne, and Mount Forest.
Highway 89 travels through rural farmland across a large part of southwestern Ontario outside of urban centres.
Highway 89 was established in 1937 as a result of the rerouting of Highway 9 between Orangeville and Highway 27.
In 1963, the route of Highway 89 was extended west to Palmerston.
In 1963, the route of Highway 89 was extended east to Highway 400.
Plans were conceived during the late 1970s to push Highway 89 further east to Highway 12 via Ravenshoe Road.
The plans to extend Highway 89 further east resulted in a brief extension to Highway 11.
Environmental protest over the chosen route through the Holland Marsh resulted in the cancellation of the extension plans in 1986.
In 1997, the section of Highway 89 between Highway 400 and Highway 11 was decommissioned.
The most recent change to the route of Highway 89 took place in 2003.
In 2003, the section of Highway 89 between Palmerston and Harriston was renumbered as part of Highway 23.
The renumbering in 2003 created a shared terminus at a junction with Highway 9.
The route of Highway 89 forms the main streets of several small towns that dot the highway east to west.
The small towns along Highway 89 include Cookstown, Alliston, and Shelburne.
Highway 89 forms the backbone of many small villages and hamlets between larger centres.
The small villages and hamlets along Highway 89 include Conn, Keldon, Primrose, Violet Hill, Rosemont, and Nicolston.
Highway 89 formerly continued past its current eastern terminus at Highway 400 to Yonge Street in the hamlet of Fennell.
The section of Highway 89 that formerly continued to Yonge Street is now numbered as Simcoe County Route 3.
Simcoe County Route 3 is known locally as Shore Acres Drive.
Highway 89 formerly continued past its current western terminus in Harriston to Palmerston.
The route to Palmerston that Highway 89 formerly took is now numbered as Highway 23.
Highway 89 mostly runs through farmland and small communities.
Highway 89 passes by Earl Rowe Provincial Park.
Highway 89 passes by the Honda car manufacturing plant in the Alliston area.
Boyne Valley Provincial Park is close to the route of Highway 89.
Mono Cliffs Provincial Park is close to the route of Highway 89.
Boyne Valley Provincial Park and Mono Cliffs Provincial Park are located on the Niagara Escarpment.
The Luther Marsh Conservation Area is a vast wilderness area that surrounds Luther Lake.
Highway 89 was created out of a highway rerouting in the late 1930s.
Highway 89 originally formed the routing of Highway 9.
Highway 9 turned north at Orangeville before Highway 89 was established.
Highway 9 travelled concurrently with Highway 10 before turning east to Cookstown.
On February 10, 1937, Highway 9 was rerouted along its present course east of Orangeville.
By 1938, Highway 89 was designated along the former route of Highway 9.
Highway 89 remained unchanged until the early 1960s.
In the early 1960s, Highway 89 was extended west to Palmerston.
In the early 1960s, Highway 89 was extended east to Highway 400.
On April 1, 1963, Highway 89 was assumed through the counties of Dufferin, Grey, and Wellington.
The section of Highway 89 between Highway 27 and Highway 400 was assumed the day after April 1, 1963.
During the mid-1970s, Highway 89 was extended east to Highway 11 at Fennell.
The section of Highway 89 that was extended east to Highway 11 was returned to the jurisdiction of Simcoe County on April 1, 1997.
During the spring of 2003, the Ministry of Transportation of Ontario renumbered several highways to improve route continuity.
The renumbering in 2003 included a section of Highway 89 between Harriston and Palmerston.
The result of the renumbering in 2003 was a shared terminus between Highway 89 and Highway 23 at an intersection with Highway 9.
During the late 1970s, plans arose to create a new highway link on the south side of Lake Simcoe.
The new highway link was intended to connect Highway 400 and Highway 12.
The route for the extension was announced on June 30, 1978.
The extension was to follow 11th Line from Highway 400 east to the Holland Marsh.
The extension would cross towards the northeast onto the alignment of Ravenshoe Road.
The extension would traverse the length of Ravenshoe Road to Lakeridge Road.
The extension would zig-zag onto Concession Road 7 to end immediately north of Sunderland.
Heavy environmental protests ensued over the chosen route through the marsh.
Transportation Minister Ed Fulton officially cancelled the extension on April 21, 1986.
The proposal for the extension has since been reborn as the Bradford Bypass.
