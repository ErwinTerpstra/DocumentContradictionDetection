The 1806 Great Coastal hurricane was a severe and damaging storm along the East Coast of the United States. The hurricane produced upwards of 36 inches (91 cm) of rainfall in parts of Massachusetts. The hurricane was first observed east of the Lesser Antilles on 17 August. The hurricane arrived at the Bahamas by 19 August. The hurricane made landfall at the mouth of the Cape Fear River in North Carolina on 22 August. The hurricane moved out to sea as a Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale. The hurricane persisted off of New England before dissipating south of Nova Scotia on 25 August. Several French military ships were damaged out at sea. Several British military ships were damaged out at sea. The salt industry in the Carolinas suffered considerably due to the hurricane. The sugar industry in the Carolinas suffered considerably due to the hurricane. The rice industry in the Carolinas suffered considerably due to the hurricane. The lumber industry in the Carolinas suffered considerably due to the hurricane. Several individuals were killed due to the hurricane. Wharves endured moderate damage due to the hurricane. Vessels endured moderate damage due to the hurricane. Many ships were wrecked on North Carolinan barrier islands due to the hurricane. A majority of the deaths caused by the hurricane occurred aboard the Rose-in-Bloom offshore of Barnegat Inlet, New Jersey. Twenty-one of the Rose-in-Bloom's 48 passengers were killed. The damage to the cargo of the Rose-in-Bloom was $171,000 (1806 USD). No deaths were reported in New England upon the hurricane's arrival. The hurricane killed more than 24 individuals along the entirety of its track.
The Great Coastal hurricane of 1806 was first noted far east of the Lesser Antilles on 17 August. Weather historian David M. Ludlum followed the disturbance's track to the Bahamas by 19 August. Intense winds persisted until 21 August approximately 150 miles (240 km) east of the Bahamian island of Eleuthera. Steering currents brought the storm northward. The hurricane approached Charleston, South Carolina on 22 August. A generally easterly flow preceded the storm's passage far east of Charleston, South Carolina. The hurricane made landfall at the mouth of the Cape Fear River in North Carolina on 22 August. The earliest impacts from the storm started several days earlier. Gusts initially shifted toward the northeast but later curved southwestward. Reports of similar wind shifts throughout the region suggested that the gale persisted stationary for several hours. The hurricane moved back out to sea while south of Norfolk, Virginia. The hurricane departed the region on 24 August. The hurricane maintained 1-minute maximum sustained winds of 110 mph (175 km/h) while offshore. The hurricane was equivalent to a Category 2 system on the Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale while offshore. The gale featured a swath of winds 90 miles (150 km) wide while offshore New England. The hurricane was last observed just south of Nova Scotia on 25 August. The hurricane was slightly weaker with sustained winds of 75 mph (120 km/h) when last observed.
