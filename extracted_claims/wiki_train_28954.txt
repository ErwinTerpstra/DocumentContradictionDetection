State Route 100 (SR 100) is a 4.68-mile-long (7.53 km) state highway in the U.S. state of Washington.
State Route 100 serves Cape Disappointment State Park in Pacific County.
State Route 100 travels counterclockwise from U.S. Route 101 (US 101) in Ilwaco south to Cape Disappointment.
State Route 100 travels north to an intersection with itself in Ilwaco.
State Route 100 is part of the Lewis and Clark Trail Scenic Byway.
State Route 100 serves as a loop route.
State Route 100 has a spur route that serves the state park.
State Route 100 has a spur route that serves a Coast Guard station.
State Route 100 was established in 1991.
State Route 100 was established on the existing North Head Road.
North Head Road was a paved county road by the late 1950s.
The highway was washed away during a 1994 winter storm.
The spur route of State Route 100 was shortened in 2006.
State Route 100 begins at an intersection with US 101 in Ilwaco.
State Route 100 turns north onto 1st Avenue towards Seaview.
State Route 100 travels west on Spruce Street.
State Route 100 has an intersection with the clockwise terminus of State Route 100 on 2nd Avenue.
State Route 100 continues towards the Pacific Ocean on North Head Road.
State Route 100 turns south along the Pacific coast past North Head Lighthouse.
State Route 100 enters Cape Disappointment State Park on Robert Gray Drive.
State Route 100 intersects its spur route that travels south on Fort Canby Road towards Coast Guard Station Cape Disappointment.
State Route 100 turns north and travels into Ilwaco along Baker Bay.
State Route 100 travels along the Columbia River before it ends at an intersection with State Route 100.
State Route 100 ends a block from its terminus at US 101.
The Washington State Department of Transportation (WSDOT) conducts a series of surveys on its highways in the state every year.
WSDOT measures traffic volume in terms of average annual daily traffic (AADT).
AADT is a measure of traffic volume for any average day of the year.
In 2011, WSDOT calculated that the busiest section of State Route 100 was its terminus at US 101 in Ilwaco.
The busiest section of State Route 100 served 2,100 vehicles.
The least busiest section of State Route 100 was the intersection with its spur route in Cape Disappointment State Park.
The least busiest section of State Route 100 served 650 vehicles.
The North Head Road portion of State Route 100 has existed as a paved roadway since 1957.

State Route 100 was made into a complete loop route around the cape.
State Route 100 has a short spur route traveling south into Fort Canby State Park.

Robert Gray Way was rebuilt after a 1994 winter storm washed away the roadway into Baker Bay.

The spur route travels south from the State Route 100 mainline to the boundary of Cape Disappointment State Park.
The spur route was established in 1991 along with the main route.
The spur route was originally 0.61 miles (0.98 km) long.
