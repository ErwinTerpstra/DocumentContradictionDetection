The FBI has confirmed that one of its most wanted terrorists, the Malaysian bomb maker known as Marwan, was killed in a raid in the Philippines in January.
Marwan's real name is Zulkifli bin Hir.
The FBI believed that Zulkifli bin Hir was a member of the southeast Asian terror group Jemaah Islamiyah's central command.
The FBI said in February that a DNA sample taken from a man killed in a raid in the southern Philippines showed a link with a known relative of Zulkifli bin Hir.
The FBI now says tests have confirmed that the dead man was the wanted Islamic extremist Zulkifli bin Hir.
David Bowdich, assistant director in charge of the FBI's Los Angeles field office, stated that Zulkifli bin Hir is deceased.
Zulkifli bin Hir has been removed from the FBI's list of Most Wanted Terrorists.
The FBI had been offering a $5 million reward for information leading to Zulkifli bin Hir's capture.
The FBI accused Zulkifli bin Hir of being a supplier of IEDs to terrorist organizations.
The FBI accused Zulkifli bin Hir of having conducted bomb making training for terror groups, including the Philippines-based Abu Sayyaf.
Zulkifli bin Hir had previously been falsely reported dead after a raid by Philippine security forces in 2012.
The Philippines has been fighting an insurgency in the predominantly Muslim south for years.
The Philippines signed a peace agreement with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF), the largest rebel group in the region, last year.
In January, the Philippines launched a surprise raid in pursuit of Zulkifli bin Hir at Mamapasono, in the southern province of Maguindanao.
The mission to capture Zulkifli bin Hir went disastrously awry.
Forty-four members of the police's elite Special Action Force (SAF) unit were killed in the assault.
The assault targeted an area controlled by the Bangsamoro Islamic Freedom Fighters (BIFF).
The BIFF is a hardline splinter group that has rejected the peace deal with the Philippines government.
The Institute for Policy Analysis of Conflict (IPAC) released a report last month stating that the BIFF faction sheltering Zulkifli bin Hir had sworn allegiance to ISIS.
In the immediate aftermath of the assault, the SAF company charged with executing Zulkifli bin Hir came under fire.
Another SAF company stationed in nearby MILF territory became engaged in an eight-hour firefight with MILF fighters.
The SAF "blocking" company eventually ran out of ammunition.
Only one member of the SAF "blocking" company survived.
Eighteen MILF fighters were killed in the clash.
A number of BIFF fighters may also have died in the clash.
The clash shattered a three-year ceasefire with the MILF.
A national day of mourning was declared as the fallen SAF members were laid to rest.
David Bowdich expressed the FBI's congratulations to the brave officers of the Special Action Force who lost their lives while attempting to apprehend Zulkifli bin Hir.
An SAF superintendent stated at a eulogy for the fallen commandos that their sacrifice had been worth it.
Controversy has dogged the botched mission in the Philippines.
The IPAC report argued that the focus of authorities on killing Zulkifli bin Hir has threatened the peace agreement with the MILF.
The peace agreement with the MILF has yet to cross the final hurdle of being passed into law by the Philippines Congress.
The IPAC report stated that the best chance the southern Philippines has ever had for peace may now be in jeopardy.
The IPAC report argued that the Mamapasano fiasco was the result of a misguided emphasis on killing Zulkifli bin Hir.
The IPAC report argued that Zulkifli bin Hir had aided terror attacks and provided funds and equipment to MILF and Abu Sayyaf.
The IPAC report stated that Zulkifli bin Hir was not "the master bomber that his reputation suggested."
Killing Zulkifli bin Hir had become a priority for the Philippines authorities and their U.S. allies.
Security forces bypassed the mechanisms that had been established to alert the MILF to such operations.
The security forces bypassed the mechanisms for fear of word leaking to Zulkifli bin Hir.
An anonymous Indonesian associate of Zulkifli bin Hir described him as "a little snake who has been blown up into a dragon."
