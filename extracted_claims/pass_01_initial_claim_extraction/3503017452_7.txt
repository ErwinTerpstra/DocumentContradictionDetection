State Route 61 (SR-61) is a nearly 7.3-mile-long (11.7 km) state highway in the U.S. state of Utah.
State Route 61 (SR-61) connects State Route 23 (SR-23) in Cornish, Cache County to U.S. Route 91 (US-91) near Richmond.
State Route 61 (SR-61) passes through Lewiston.
State Route 61 (SR-61) is located in the extreme northern part of Utah.
State Route 61 (SR-61) has existed since at least 1914.
State Route 61 (SR-61) has been designated as such since at least 1937.
Between 735 and 2,180 vehicles travel along State Route 61 (SR-61) on an average day in 2012.
At the intersection of State Route 23 (SR-23) and 13400 North in the center of Cornish, State Route 61 (SR-61) departs east on 13400 North.
State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses over a single track belonging to the Union Pacific Railroad (UP).
State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses the Bear River.
State Route 61 (SR-61) continues east through rural Cache County.
State Route 61 (SR-61) intersects State Route 200 (SR-200), a connector road to Preston, Idaho.
The shoulder of State Route 61 (SR-61) is up to four feet (1.2 m) wide from the western terminus to State Route 200 (SR-200).
The remainder of State Route 61 (SR-61) has shoulders that are between less than or equal to one foot (0.3 m) and nine-tenths feet (0.58 m) wide.
The name of State Route 61 (SR-61) changes from 13400 South to Center Street through Lewiston.
State Route 61 (SR-61) passes the Lewiston Cemetery.
State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses over the Cub River.
State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses a second single track belonging to UP.
State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses a third single track belonging to UP just before the highway's eastern terminus at U.S. Route 91 (US-91) north of Richmond.
All of the rail lines that State Route 61 (SR-61) crosses originally belonged to the Oregon Short Line Railway.
Aside from the segment through Lewiston, State Route 61 (SR-61) is surrounded by farmland for its entire journey across northern Utah.
The Utah Department of Transportation (UDOT) conducts a series of surveys on its highways in the state every year to measure traffic volume.
Traffic volume is expressed in terms of average annual daily traffic (AADT).
As few as 500 vehicles used State Route 61 (SR-61) on an average day at its western terminus in Cornish.
Thirty-five percent of the traffic on State Route 61 (SR-61) at its western terminus in Cornish was truck traffic.
A roadway linking Cornish to the east has existed since at least 1914.
The roadway that serves as the eastern terminus was numbered State Route 1 (SR-1) by 1927.
The highway has been officially designated as State Route 61 (SR-61) since at least 1937.
The 53-foot-long (16.2 m) bridge that carries State Route 61 (SR-61) over the Cub River was constructed in 1952.
The 182-foot-long (55.5 m) bridge over the Bear River was built in 1961.
The original river crossings of State Route 61 (SR-61) were slightly further south than their current locations.
The entire route of State Route 61 (SR-61) is in Cache County.
