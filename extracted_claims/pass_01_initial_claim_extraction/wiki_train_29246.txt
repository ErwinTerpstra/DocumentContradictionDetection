M - 188 is a 4.559-mile-long (7.337 km) state trunkline highway in the US state of Michigan.
M - 188 runs from Eaton Rapids to the VFW National Home for Children.
M - 188 runs southeast of Eaton Rapids through a rural area.
Approximately 1,000 vehicles each day use M - 188.
M - 188 was first designated in the 1930s.
M - 188 was paved in the 1940s.
M - 188 starts in Eaton Rapids.
M - 188 heads eastward from the intersection with M - 99 / M - 50.
M - 188 heads toward the Grand River.
M - 188 follows Water Street southeasterly along the Grand River.
M - 188 runs through residential neighborhoods.
M - 188 exits Eaton Rapids.
M - 188 turns back due east on VFW Road.
M - 188 runs through farm fields.
M - 188 turns south near the intersection with Tucker Road.
M - 188 turns southeasterly to avoid a bend in the Grand River.
VFW Road runs due east after the bend in the Grand River.
M - 188 intersects with Waverly Road.
M - 188 turns south on Waverly Road.
Waverly Road runs along the Eaton – Ingham county line.
The M - 188 designation ends at the VFW National Home for Children.
The VFW National Home for Children is about one mile (1.6 km) south of VFW Road.
M - 188 is maintained by the Michigan Department of Transportation (MDOT).
MDOT tracks the volume of traffic that uses the roadways under its jurisdiction.
Traffic volumes are expressed using a metric called annual average daily traffic.
Annual average daily traffic is a statistical calculation of the average daily number of vehicles on a segment of roadway.
MDOT's surveys in 2010 showed that 986 vehicles traveled along M - 188 in the city of Eaton Rapids on average.
MDOT's surveys in 2010 showed that 1,103 vehicles traveled along M - 188 outside of Eaton Rapids on average.
M - 188 has not been listed on the National Highway System.
The National Highway System is a network of roads important to the country's economy, defense, and mobility.
M - 188 was designated in late 1932.
M - 188 provided highway access to the VFW National Home from Eaton Rapids.
M - 188 is located in southeast Eaton County.
M - 188 was completely paved in early 1941.
M - 188 has been unchanged since it was completely paved.
