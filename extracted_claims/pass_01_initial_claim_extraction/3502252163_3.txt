M - 82 is a state trunkline in the Lower Peninsula in the US state of Michigan.
M - 82 travels between Fremont and Howard City.
The section of M - 82 between Newaygo and Howard City travels through Fremont.
The section of M - 82 between Newaygo and Howard City travels along the southern edge of Manistee National Forest.
The Lower Peninsula routing of M - 82 has not been in use since the 1920s.
The current version of M - 82 is the second version in the state of Michigan.
The first usage of M - 82 appeared in the Upper Peninsula by 1919.
The Lower Peninsula routing of M - 82 has been in use since the 1920s.
Various extensions and truncations have shifted the terminus of M - 82 as far west as New Era or Hesperia in the past.
The current route of M - 82 was finalized in the late 1970s.
M - 82 begins at a junction with M - 120 and B - 96 west of Fremont.
The junction of M - 82 with M - 120 and B - 96 is at a tripoint of county lines.
M - 120 forms the north-south Newaygo-Oceana county line.
M - 120 forms the north-south Newaygo-Muskegon county line.
B - 96 forms the east-west Oceana-Muskegon county line.
B - 96 runs due west of the intersection with M - 82.
M - 82 exits the intersection with M - 120 and B - 96 to the east.
M - 82 runs along 48th Street through rural farms into the community of Fremont.
The road, now called Main Street, passes through the downtown area of Fremont.
M - 82 turns south along Stewart Avenue out of the downtown area of Fremont.
M - 82 rounds a curve and transitions to 72nd Street running eastward through more farmland.
As M - 82 approaches Newaygo, the terrain becomes more wooded near the Muskegon River.
M - 82 follows Fremont Street east to a junction with M - 37.
M - 37 merges with M - 82 and they run concurrently over the river into downtown on State Street.
South of downtown, M - 82 turns east again, independent of M - 37.
M - 82 runs along 82nd Street on the southern edge of the Manistee National Forest.
The east end of M - 82 is at exit 118 on US 131 / M - 46 west of Howard City.
The Michigan Department of Transportation (MDOT) tracks the volume of traffic using its roadways.
MDOT expresses traffic levels in terms of average annual daily traffic (AADT).
AADT is a calculation of the traffic along a segment of road for any average day of the year.
In 2009, MDOT's measurements indicated that a segment of M - 82 west of Fremont had a peak volume of 16,532 vehicles daily.
The traffic nadir on M - 82 was 4,018 vehicles east of Newaygo.
MDOT has not had any section of M - 82 listed on the National Highway System.
The first usage of M - 82 in the Upper Peninsula was by July 1, 1919.
The first usage of M - 82 started at M - 25.
The first usage of M - 82 ran north of Newberry to the vicinity of Eight Mile Corner.
M - 82 was replaced by M - 48 in 1926.
The current M - 82 dates back to 1926.
The current M - 82 ran from US 31 in Hart to the northern junction of US 131 and M - 46 in Howard City.
The current M - 82 was routed through Ferry, Hesperia, and Fremont.
The current M - 82 replaced M - 41.
In late 1936, M - 46 was extended along the section between Newaygo and Howard City.
The extension of M - 46 formed a concurrency with M - 82 to fill a gap in the M - 46 routing.
The concurrent section of M - 46 and M - 82 became just M - 46 in 1938.
The change in 1938 shortened M - 82 back to the northern M - 37 junction in Newaygo.
The highway was moved to a new alignment west of Ferry in late 1947 or early 1948.
The new alignment of M - 82 continued west to end in Shelby.
Two realignments of M - 82 occurred in 1963 and 1964.
The realignments rerouted the western end of M - 82 to run from Hesperia to New Era.
The realignments bypassed Ferry.
A larger change around 1969 reconfigured the highway designations in Oceana County.
M - 20 replaced M - 82 west of Hesperia.
M - 20 ended at New Era instead of Muskegon.
The new M - 120 designation replaced M - 20 south of Hesperia.
M - 82 ran from Hesperia to Newaygo only after the changes.
The length of M - 82 was increased in 1973.
The eastern end of M - 82 was moved back to Howard City in 1973.
M - 46 was rerouted to follow the US 131 freeway south of Howard City to Cedar Springs.
M - 46 replaced M - 57 between Cedar Springs and Casnovia.
The changes in 1973 freed up the Newaygo to Howard City highway for a return to the M - 82 designation.
The last change to M - 82 occurred in 1978.
The concurrency with M - 120 was eliminated in favor of M - 120 in 1978.
The M - 82 designation was truncated at the junction west of Fremont in 1978.
The truncation resulted in the current highway routing of M - 82.
