Krasnyi Kavkaz was a cruiser of the Soviet Navy.
Krasnyi Kavkaz began construction during World War I.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was incomplete during the Russian Revolution.
The design of Krasnyi Kavkaz was heavily modified by the Soviets.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was completed in 1932.
Krasnyi Kavkaz supported Soviet troops during the Siege of Odessa.
Krasnyi Kavkaz supported Soviet troops during the Siege of Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz supported Soviet troops during the Kerch-Feodosiya Operation in the winter of 1941-42.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was awarded the Guards title on 3 April 1942.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was reclassified as a training ship in May 1947.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was used as a target in 1952.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was laid down on 18 October 1913 at the Rossud Dockyard.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was originally named Admiral Lazarev.
Admiral Lazarev was a cruiser of the Svetlana class.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was launched on 8 June 1916.
Construction of Krasnyi Kavkaz was abandoned in 1917 during the October Revolution.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was 63% complete when construction was abandoned.
The Marine Department of Hetman Pavlo Skoropadskyi was engaged in the completion of Krasnyi Kavkaz in the second half of 1918.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was formally renamed Hetman Petro Doroshenko on 25 January 1919.
Mykolaiv was captured shortly after Hetman Petro Doroshenko was renamed.
The hull of Hetman Petro Doroshenko was relatively undamaged.
The Soviets decided to finish Hetman Petro Doroshenko to a modified design.
The design of Hetman Petro Doroshenko was not modified by the Soviets.
Hetman Petro Doroshenko was renamed Krasnyi Kavkaz on 14 December 1926.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was completed to a modernized design.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was commissioned on 25 January 1932.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was initially intended to accommodate eight 8-inch (200 mm) guns in four twin turrets.
Krasnyi Kavkaz could not accommodate eight 8-inch (200 mm) guns due to her small and lightly constructed hull.
Krasnyi Kavkaz had to settle for four MK-1-180 single 180 mm gun turrets.
Krasnyi Kavkaz had two MK-1-180 single 180 mm gun turrets at each end.
The superstructure of Krasnyi Kavkaz was massively revised to fit the turrets.
All of the original casemated 130-millimeter (5.1 in) / 55 B7 Pattern 1913 guns were removed from Krasnyi Kavkaz.
Krasnyi Kavkaz had only four 30-caliber 76.2 mm Lender AA guns mounted between her funnels as completed.
The original internal torpedo tubes of Krasnyi Kavkaz were replaced by four triple 533-millimeter (21.0 in) torpedo mounts.
The four triple 533-millimeter (21.0 in) torpedo mounts were mounted on each side of the main deck abaft the forecastle break.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was given an aircraft-handling crane.
A catapult was not installed aft of Krasnyi Kavkaz's rear funnel until 1935.
A Heinkel catapult was imported from Germany for Krasnyi Kavkaz in 1935.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was fitted for mine rails with a capacity of up to 120 mines.
The light cruiser Komintern collided with Krasnyi Kavkaz in May 1932.
The collision with Komintern badly damaged the bow of Krasnyi Kavkaz.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was extensively rebuilt after the collision.
The rebuilding of Krasnyi Kavkaz increased her overall length by over 11 meters (36 ft).
Krasnyi Kavkaz made port visits in Turkey, Greece, and Italy in 1933.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was refitted before Operation Barbarossa.
Krasnyi Kavkaz's catapult was removed before Operation Barbarossa.
The anti-aircraft armament of Krasnyi Kavkaz was greatly increased before Operation Barbarossa.
Krasnyi Kavkaz exchanged her four 76.2 mm Lender AA guns for four Italian Minizini twin gun 50-caliber 100 mm AA mounts.
Krasnyi Kavkaz received four single mounts for the semi-automatic 76.2 mm 34-K guns.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was fitted with six 12.7 mm (0.50 in) AA machine guns.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was fitted with two single mounts for 76.2 mm (3.00 in) 34-K guns.
One single mount for a 76.2 mm (3.00 in) 34-K gun was fitted on each side of the quarterdeck just aft of the rearmost main gun turret.
Some of the guns on Krasnyi Kavkaz may have been received earlier.
The sources regarding the earlier receipt of guns for Krasnyi Kavkaz are unclear.
Krasnyi Kavkaz landed her aft pair of torpedo tubes while under repair at Poti in late 1942.
Krasnyi Kavkaz received two more Minizini mounts salvaged from the sunken cruiser Chervona Ukraina.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was fitted with ten single mounts for the naval version of the 37 mm AA gun.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was also carrying one quadruple Vickers .50 machine gun MK III mount on top of each of her superfiring main gun turrets by 1944.
Krasnyi Kavkaz may have been using Oerlikon 20 mm cannon by 1944.
Krasnyi Kavkaz laid down a defensive mine barrage protecting the Black Sea Fleet base at Sevastopol on 22 June.
Krasnyi Kavkaz provided gunfire support to Soviet forces defending Odessa in September 1941.
Krasnyi Kavkaz escorted convoys bringing the 157th Rifle Division into Odessa during September 1941.
Krasnyi Kavkaz transported one battalion of the 3rd Marine Regiment from Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz participated in a successful amphibious assault behind Romanian lines to destroy Romanian coastal batteries near Fontanka and Dofinovka.
Krasnyi Kavkaz escorted convoys from 3-6 October that evacuated the 157th Rifle Division from Odessa to Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz escorted the final evacuation convoy during the night of 15-16 October.
Krasnyi Kavkaz provided gunfire support during the Siege of Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz evacuated cut-off troops from elsewhere in the Crimea into Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz brought in reinforcements from Caucasian ports during the Siege of Sevastopol.
Krasnyi Kavkaz helped to transport the 388th Rifle Division from Novorossiysk and Tuapse to Sevastopol between 7 and 13 December.
Krasnyi Kavkaz transported the 354th Rifle Division between 21 and 22 December.
Krasnyi Kavkaz bombarded German positions in the interim between transporting the 388th and 354th Rifle Divisions.
Krasnyi Kavkaz sailed into the harbor of Feodosiya on 29 December 1941.
Krasnyi Kavkaz disembarked reinforcements in Feodosiya.
Krasnyi Kavkaz provided gunfire support for Soviet troops already ashore in Feodosiya.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was hit seventeen times by Axis artillery and mortar fire in retaliation.
Krasnyi Kavkaz ferried supplies and reinforcements for the Soviet bridgehead on the Kerch Peninsula on 1 and 3 January.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was severely damaged by German Junkers Ju 87 Stuka dive-bombers from II./StG 77 on the return voyage.
Four near-misses close to Krasnyi Kavkaz's stern damaged her steering.
The left propeller shaft of Krasnyi Kavkaz was damaged by the near-misses.
One propeller of Krasnyi Kavkaz was blown off by the near-misses.
The near-misses put enough holes in Krasnyi Kavkaz's stern that flooding caused her draft to increase by 5 meters (16 ft).
Krasnyi Kavkaz made it to Novorossiysk escorted by the destroyer Sposobnyi.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was patched up enough in Novorossiysk to make it to Poti.
More permanent repairs for Krasnyi Kavkaz could be made in Poti.
The repairs for Krasnyi Kavkaz took until October 1942.
The opportunity was taken to reinforce the anti-aircraft armament of Krasnyi Kavkaz during repairs.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was awarded the Guards title on 3 April in recognition of her performance.
Between 20 and 23 October, Krasnyi Kavkaz ferried 12,600 men of the 8th, 9th, and 10th Guards Rifle Brigades from Poti to Tuapse.
Krasnyi Kavkaz, her half-sister Krasnyi Krym, and three destroyers provided fire support for the main landing on the night of 4 February 1943.
The Soviet troops at the main landing on the night of 4 February 1943 were wiped out by 6 February.
One secondary landing during the amphibious landings was successful.
The loss of three destroyers attempting to interdict the German evacuation of the Taman Bridgehead on 6 October 1943 caused Stalin to forbid the deployment of large naval units without his express permission.
The order from Stalin meant the end of Krasnyi Kavkaz's active participation in the war.
Little is known about the activities of Krasnyi Kavkaz after the end of the war.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was redesignated as a training ship on 12 May 1947.
Krasnyi Kavkaz was sunk as a target ship by SS-N-1 missiles on 21 November 1952.
