Mississippi Highway 9W (MS 9W) is a 20.3-mile (32.7 km) long state highway located in northern Mississippi.
The southern terminus of Mississippi Highway 9W is at MS 9 north of Bruce in Calhoun County.
The northern terminus of Mississippi Highway 9W is at MS 7 south of Oxford in Lafayette County.
Mississippi Highway 9W is a two-lane undivided road its entire length.
Mississippi Highway 9W passes through rural areas of woods and farms.
Mississippi Highway 9W intersects MS 315 in Paris.
Mississippi Highway 9W was designated to follow its current alignment in 1956.
Mississippi Highway 9W was fully paved from a gravel road by 1960.
Mississippi Highway 9W begins at an intersection with MS 9 to the north of Bruce in Calhoun County.
Mississippi Highway 9W heads northwest on a two-lane undivided road through a mix of woods and farmlands with a few homes.
Mississippi Highway 9W turns to the north and reaches the community of Banner.
Mississippi Highway 9W crosses two County Roads: CR 275 and CR 267 in Banner.
Mississippi Highway 9W enters a dense forest as it curves northwest.
Mississippi Highway 9W heads into more agricultural fields.
Mississippi Highway 9W comes to a junction with CR 284.
Mississippi Highway 9W crosses into Lafayette County.
Mississippi Highway 9W reaches the community of Paris.
Mississippi Highway 9W intersects the eastern terminus of MS 315 in Paris.
Mississippi Highway 9W intersects CR 428 in Paris.
After the intersection with CR 428, Mississippi Highway 9W passes through several more miles of forests.
Mississippi Highway 9W intersects CR 371 before reaching its terminus at MS 7 to the south of Oxford.
Mississippi Highway 9W is legally defined in Mississippi Code § 65-3-3.
Mississippi Highway 9W was designated in 1956 for a gravel road connecting MS 9 north of Bruce to MS 7 south of Oxford.
By 1960, the entire route of Mississippi Highway 9W was paved.
Since 1960, Mississippi Highway 9W has not undergone any alignment changes.
