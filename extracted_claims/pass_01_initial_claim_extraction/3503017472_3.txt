State Route 319 (SR 319) is a very short, two-lane state highway in the western portion of the U.S. state of Ohio.
SR 319 has its western terminus at an abandoned railroad grade just west of Washington Street in the village of Burkettsville.
SR 319 has its eastern terminus 0.56 miles (0.90 km) to the east of its western terminus at its junction with SR 118.
SR 319 was established in the late 1950s.
SR 319 started out as a much longer highway.
Earlier segments of SR 319 became a part of SR 118 in the late 1930s.
SR 319 runs along the boundary between Darke County on the south side and Mercer County on the north side for its entire length.
SR 319 begins in downtown Burkettsville at a former railroad grade just west of Washington Street.
SR 319 is known as Main Street within Burkettsville.
SR 319 runs due east through Burkettsville's central business district.
SR 319 enters a residential area where it intersects a few side streets.
After leaving the village of Burkettsville, SR 319 becomes the boundary between Darke County's Allen Township and Mercer County's Granville Township.
After a short distance through farmland, SR 319 comes to an end at its junction with SR 118.
Continuing east after SR 319 terminates is Darke – Mercer County Line Road.
The Ohio Department of Transportation (ODOT) conducts surveys throughout its highway system to determine traffic volume.
Traffic volume is most commonly expressed as the average annual daily traffic (AADT).
In 2010, ODOT determined that an average of 710 vehicles per day traveled along the length of SR 319.
SR 319 is not included as a part of the National Highway System.
The National Highway System is a system of highways identified as being most important for the economy, mobility, and defense of the nation.
SR 319 was assigned in 1932.
Originally, SR 319 included the entirety of the current route.
SR 319 originally included the stretch of SR 118 from SR 319 north to SR 219 in Coldwater.
In 1935, SR 319 was extended further north along the present alignment of SR 118.
The present alignment of SR 118 was then designated as SR 32.
SR 32 is now a part of SR 29, west of Celina.
In 1937, SR 118 was extended south from its former southern terminus in Rockford to its present southern terminus in Greenville.
The entirety of what was the north-south stretch of SR 319 prior to 1937 became a part of the new extension of SR 118.
SR 319 became the short spur route that it is today.
