The Zrinski Battalion was a special forces unit of the Croatian National Guard.
The Zrinski Battalion was later a unit of the Croatian Army.
The Zrinski Battalion was established in Kumrovec on 18 May 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion was established during the Croatian War of Independence.
The Zrinski Battalion drew personnel from the special police forces.
The Zrinski Battalion drew personnel from former French Foreign Legion troops.
Ante Roso was the initial commander of the Zrinski Battalion.
Major Miljenko Filipović took over as the commanding officer of the Zrinski Battalion in August 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion trained volunteer troops in Vukovar in June 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion saw action in Hrvatska Kostajnica in 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion saw action in the Battle of Gospić in 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion saw action near Slano in 1991.
By the end of 1991, the personnel of the Zrinski Battalion were tasked with setting up an additional special forces unit of the Croatian Army.
Elements of the Zrinski Battalion took part in the Battle of Kupres in 1992.
Elements of the Zrinski Battalion took part in Operation Tiger in 1992.
Operation Tiger aimed at lifting the Siege of Dubrovnik.
The Zrinski Battalion helped develop and train the Croatian Defence Council.
The Zrinski Battalion set up a training camp in Tomislavgrad.
The Zrinski Battalion took part in Operation Maslenica in 1993.
The Zrinski Battalion was amalgamated with several other special forces units on 25 February 1994.
The Zrinski Battalion was amalgamated into the 1st Croatian Guards Brigade.
The 1st Croatian Guards Brigade is a component of the 1st Croatian Guards Corps.
The 1st Croatian Guards Brigade is directly subordinated to the Ministry of Defence.
The Zrinski Battalion was based in the village of Kumrovec.
The Zrinski Battalion was based on the grounds of the former "Josip Broz Tito" political school.
The site of the Zrinski Battalion was selected to be inaccessible to Yugoslav Air Force raids.
The Kumrovec Special Police Unit was transferred to Sljeme Peak north of Zagreb in June 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion was deployed for the first time on 15 June 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion was stationed in Vukovar.
The Zrinski Battalion was tasked with preparation of city defences in Vukovar.
The Zrinski Battalion was tasked with organisation of volunteer troops in Vukovar.
Filipović took over command of the Zrinski Battalion from Roso in August 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion was deployed to the Banovina in August 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion pushed the Croatian Serb forces out of the town of Hrvatska Kostajnica.
The Zrinski Battalion was deployed to Gospić in September 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion took part in the battle to control Gospić against the Yugoslav People's Army.
Troops assigned to the Zrinski Battalion captured Kaniža barracks in Gospić.
During combat in Gospić, 30 troops of the Zrinski Battalion captured JNA Major General Trajče Krstevski.
During combat in Gospić, 30 troops of the Zrinski Battalion captured three armoured personnel carriers.
During combat in Gospić, 30 troops of the Zrinski Battalion captured 32 soldiers.
The Zrinski Battalion was deployed to Metković on 28 October 1991.
The Zrinski Battalion was tasked with recapturing Slano from the Yugoslav People's Army.
A part of the Zrinski Battalion personnel left for Bosnia and Herzegovina anticipating further conflict.
The remainder of the Zrinski Battalion returned to Kumrovec after the deployment to Gospić.

Personnel of the Zrinski Battalion set up another special forces unit of the Croatian Army in late 1991.
The new special forces unit set up by the Zrinski Battalion was the Matija Vlačić Battalion.
The Matija Vlačić Battalion was based in Opatija.
