Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) is a highway in northwestern Nebraska.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) has a western terminus at Wyoming Highway 151 (WYO 151) at the Wyoming-Nebraska state line.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) travels eastward to N-71.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) turns south at N-71.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) continues east to south of Bridgeport.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) turns north at an intersection with U.S. Highway 385 (US 385) and N-92 in Bridgeport.
The route of Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) was designated in 1937.
The official state highway system was created after 1937.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) was extended to the state line in 1986.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) starts at the Nebraska-Wyoming state line in Banner County.
WYO 151 ends at the Nebraska-Wyoming state line in Banner County.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) travels northeast after starting at the Nebraska-Wyoming state line.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) bends east after less than one mile (1.6 km).
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) continues in a straight line after bending east.
For the next twenty miles (32 km), Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) intersects minor streets through rural farmland.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) becomes concurrent with N-71.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) turns east four miles (6.4 km) later.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) ends the concurrency with N-71 after turning east.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) continues to travel through farmland for sixteen miles (26 km).
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) enters Morrill County after traveling through farmland.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) crosses over Pumpkin Creek four times.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) enters the unincorporated community of Redington.
Two rock formations, Courthouse and Jail Rocks, become visible from Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88).
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) turns north toward Bridgeport soon after entering Redington.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) crosses over Pumpkin Creek for the fifth time.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) enters Bridgeport five miles (8.0 km) later.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) intersects a railroad owned by BNSF Railway.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) turns northeast soon after intersecting the railroad.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) ends at the intersection of US 385 and N-92.
In 2012, the Nebraska Department of Roads (NDOR) calculated as many as 2,410 vehicles traveling on the N-71/N-88 concurrency.
In 2012, the Nebraska Department of Roads (NDOR) calculated as few as 315 vehicles traveling east of the Banner-Morrill county line.
The traffic volume is expressed in terms of annual average daily traffic (AADT).
Only the N-71/N-88 concurrency is part of the National Highway System (NHS).
The National Highway System (NHS) is a network of highways identified as being most important for the economy, mobility, and defense of the nation.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) is a highway in southeastern Nebraska.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) was unofficially designated around 1937.
Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) connected from N-29 to N-86 and N-19 in Bridgeport.
The route of Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) remained relatively the same as the state highway system was officially designated.
Before 1955, Nebraska did not have an adequate legal instrument to define the state highway system.
By 1960, N-19 was renumbered to US 385.
By 1960, US 26 was rerouted north near Bridgeport.
The old alignment of US 26 became part of N-92.
Two years later, N-29 was renumbered to N-71.
Between 1981-82, a road appeared on the official state map extending from WYO 151 to N-71.
That road became part of Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) by 1986.
No significant changes have been made to Nebraska Highway 88 (N-88) since 1986.
