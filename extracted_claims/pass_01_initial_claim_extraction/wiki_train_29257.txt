New York State Route 286 (NY 286) is an east-west state highway in the vicinity of the city of Rochester, New York, in the United States.
New York State Route 286 links Rochester to its eastern suburbs.
New York State Route 286 links Rochester to western Wayne County.
The western terminus of New York State Route 286 is at exit 7 on NY 590 just inside the Rochester city limits.
The eastern terminus of New York State Route 286 is at NY 350 in Walworth.
New York State Route 286 is named Browncroft Boulevard from NY 590 to its junction with Qualtrough and Clark roads in Penfield.
The portion of New York State Route 286 named Browncroft Boulevard passes through predominantly residential areas.
East of the intersection with Qualtrough and Clark roads, New York State Route 286 is known as Atlantic Avenue.
New York State Route 286 traverses more rural areas east of the intersection with Qualtrough and Clark roads.
The entirety of New York State Route 286 east of Blossom Road was originally designated as part of NY 35 during the 1930 renumbering of state highways in New York.
NY 35 exited Rochester on Blossom Road.
NY 35 followed Blossom Road east to modern New York State Route 286.
The remainder of Browncroft Boulevard was designated as New York State Route 35B in the mid-1930s.
New York State Route 35 and New York State Route 35B designations were eliminated in the early 1940s.
New York State Route 35 was replaced with NY 383.
New York State Route 35B was replaced with New York State Route 383B.
NY 383 was truncated to downtown Rochester in 1949.
The former routing from Rochester to Walworth became New York State Route 286 in 1949.
New York State Route 383B was renumbered to New York State Route 286A.
The portion of Browncroft Boulevard from the Sea Breeze Expressway to Blossom Road was upgraded into a divided highway in the 1960s.
New York State Route 286 was rerouted to follow the improved Browncroft Boulevard.
New York State Route 286A was shifted south onto New York State Route 286's former alignment along Blossom Road.
The NY 286A designation was removed in the early 1970s.
Blossom Road remains state-maintained as NY 941V.
New York State Route 286 begins at the ramp connecting NY 590 southbound to Browncroft Boulevard just inside the Rochester city limits.
New York State Route 286 heads east through residential surroundings as a four-lane highway.
New York State Route 286 proceeds through the remainder of NY 590 exit 7.
New York State Route 286 enters the northernmost portion of the town of Brighton.
East of NY 590, New York State Route 286 widens to become a divided highway.
New York State Route 286 passes into Penfield upon crossing Irondequoit Creek.
New York State Route 286 curves southeast as it straddles the northern boundary of Ellison Park.
New York State Route 286 intersects with Old Browncroft Boulevard.
Old Browncroft Boulevard is the pre-divided highway alignment of Browncroft Boulevard.
Old Browncroft Boulevard is now discontinuous near its midpoint.
The two highways loosely parallel each other southeastward for 0.75 miles (1.21 km) to White Village Drive.
White Village Drive is a residential side street branching off Browncroft Boulevard.
Old Browncroft Boulevard ends at White Village Drive.
New York State Route 286 narrows slightly to become a four-lane undivided highway after White Village Drive.
New York State Route 286 continues eastward, leaving the Ellison Park area.
New York State Route 286 intersects Blossom Road.
East of the junction with Blossom Road, New York State Route 286 takes on a linear east-west routing.
New York State Route 286 becomes Atlantic Avenue at a junction with Qualtrough and Clark Roads.
The residential nature of New York State Route 286 largely ends at Five Mile Line Road.
The homes that have lined New York State Route 286 become more sporadic at Five Mile Line Road.
Open cultivated fields replace homes along New York State Route 286 at Five Mile Line Road.
New York State Route 286 has a slight s-curve between Five Mile Line and Baird Roads.
New York State Route 286 reverts to its true eastward alignment prior to intersecting NY 250.
New York State Route 286 continues to the east toward the Monroe-Wayne county line.
Just before crossing into Wayne County, New York State Route 286 curves slightly to the northeast.
In Walworth, New York State Route 286 turns back to the east at a junction with County Line Road.
County Line Road is located 0.3 miles (0.5 km) east of the official border.
Farther east, New York State Route 286 intersects several local major arterials.
The local major arterials include West Walworth Road and Canandaigua Road.
New York State Route 286 heads over several small hills.
New York State Route 286 passes through a mixture of open fields and forested areas.
New York State Route 286 and Atlantic Avenue both come to an end at an intersection with NY 350.
The portion of New York State Route 286 in Wayne County is maintained by the county as County Route 203.
In the 1930 renumbering of state highways in New York, NY 35 was extended northeast from Canawaugus to Ontario.
NY 35 entered Rochester on Scottsville Road.
NY 35 followed Genesee and Main streets through the city of Rochester.
At the east end of Main Street, NY 35 turned south and followed Winton Road to Blossom Road.
NY 35 curved eastward to follow Blossom Road east to Browncroft Boulevard in Penfield.
An alternate route of NY 35 along the portion of Browncroft Boulevard between Winton and Blossom Roads was designated NY 35B.
The NY 35 designation remained in place until the early 1940s.
The NY 35 designation was split into two designations: NY 383 west of Walworth and NY 350 from Walworth to Ontario.
NY 35B was renumbered to NY 383B as part of the change.
NY 383 was altered on January 1, 1949, to end in downtown Rochester.
The former routing of NY 383 between East Avenue (NY 96) in Rochester and NY 350 in Walworth became NY 286.
NY 383B was redesignated as NY 286A.
The portion of the Sea Breeze Expressway between Empire Boulevard and Interstate 490 opened to traffic around 1961.
Both NY 286 and NY 286A were truncated to begin at their interchanges with the Sea Breeze Expressway.
In Wayne County, the routing of NY 286 was altered slightly in the vicinity of the county line.
NY 286 originally veered onto what is now Knollwood Drive.
NY 286 followed Knollwood Drive and County Line Road to where County Line Road met Atlantic Avenue.
A new roadway was constructed during the mid-1960s.
The new roadway directly connected the two portions of Atlantic Avenue.
The new roadway bypassed County Line Road entirely.
The new roadway became part of a realigned NY 286 by 1968.
Browncroft Boulevard was upgraded into a four-lane divided highway between the Sea Breeze Expressway and Blossom Road during the mid-1960s.
Some parts of Browncroft Boulevard were upgraded on the spot.
Other parts of Browncroft Boulevard were bypassed and are now known today as Old Browncroft Boulevard.
As a result of the upgrade, the alignments of NY 286 and NY 286A west of Blossom Road were flipped.
NY 286 was placed on Browncroft Boulevard.
NY 286A was placed on Blossom Road.
The NY 286A designation was replaced with NY 941V.
NY 941V is an unsigned reference route 2.20 miles (3.54 km) in length.
Reference markers along Blossom Road still read "286A."
